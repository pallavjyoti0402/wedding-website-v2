Exploring Sivasagar

Rang Ghar

Built in the year 1746, Rang Ghar, often called the "Colosseum of the East," is one of the oldest amphitheaters in Asia. Built by the Ahom kings, it was used for royal sports and cultural events. The structure has a distinctive roof and reflects the grandeur of Ahom architecture. The building is constructed in the shape of an inverted boat and is double storied. The walls are decorated with panels depicting scenes of animal fights, but little survives today. Some decorative motifs are still visible as one climbs to the first floor.

Talatal Ghar

Talatal Ghar is a seven-storied palace, with three of its floors underground. It served as the military base for the Ahom kings and had secret tunnels connecting to other important sites. The palace's strategic design made it a stronghold during wars.

Talatal Ghar is one of the must-visit monuments in Shibsagar. The monument was an erstwhile palace. There is a seven storeyed palace, out of which three are below the ground while the remaining are above. There are also secret tunnels said to be in existence. The Talatal Ghar was the citadel of Ahom power, built by Ahom king Rudra Sinha. The entire building and the surrounding walls were made of thin bricks, while eggshells and rice pastes were used as mortar, as lime’s functionality was unknown then.

Kareng Ghar

The Kareng Palace, also known as the Garhgaon Palace, was the royal residence of the Ahom kings. The multi-storied structure is surrounded by ramparts and offers a glimpse into the architectural grandeur of the Ahoms.

Kareng Ghar is situated around 13kms away from Shibsagar. This was the place where the Ahoms shifted their capital from Charaideo. The palace was built of wood, stone and bamboo with beautiful woodwork and motifs. The palace has not been able to withstand the test of time, and for the present, only a single portion of the palace remains.

Shiva Dol

The temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is said to be one of the tallest in the world, standing at 180 feet, built in the Year 1734 by Ahom Queen Ambika and draws enormous crowds even today. Shiva Dol is one of the most important temples in Assam, situated in Shibsagar. The temple is on the banks of the Shibsagar Lake and is flanked by two more temples, Vishnu Dol and Devi Dol. These temples are a must-visit for all who come to this part of Assam. These temples reflect the architectural brilliance of the Ahom rulers and are important pilgrimage sites in Assam.

Ahom Museum

The Ahom Museum, located on the banks of Sivasagar Lake, houses a collection of artifacts from the Ahom era, including swords, manuscripts, and royal belongings. It offers insight into the life and legacy of the Ahom rulers.

Talatal Ghar

Sivasagar Lake, also known as Borpukhuri, is an artificial lake built by Queen Ambika, wife of Ahom King Swargadeo Siva Singha. The lake is surrounded by temples, and its serene environment makes it a popular spot for visitors. Next to the main temple of Shiva Dol lies the vast and hauntingly beautiful Shibsagar Lake. It is an artificial lake built by Ahom Queen Ambika in 1734 to ensure the availability of a steady water supply.
